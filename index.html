Visit Chania

Enjoy the vibrant city life and the relaxing countryside

Must see attractions

The lighthouse

The Egyptian Lighthouse in the Venetian port of Chania constitutes an absolute jewel of the city and its most photographed monument. Despite the adjective “Egyptian” that is attributed to the Lighthouse, it was built by the Venetians in the late 16th century and is one of the oldest in the world,fact that makes it so special.

Splantzia

Splantzia is one of the most unique neighborhoods in Chania city. In the center of the neighborhood lies the scenic Splantzia square. It is a wonderful small square with big plane trees providing shade during the hot summer months.On the edges of the square there are lots of tables and chairs from the adjoining cafes and taverns.

Balos beach

Bridging the gap between the wild Gramvousa Peninsula and the idyllic Cape Tigani, lagoon of Balos has white sand and exotic white, vivid blue and turquoise waters. In many places the sand has a lovely pinkish color, because of millions of crushed shells. At the boundaries of the lagoon, the water is deeper and colder, ideal for a snorkeling.

Elafonisi beach

Elafonisi is one of the most breathtaking beaches with clear blue waters, pink sand, and unique landscape . It has been ranked as one of the most beautiful beaches in the world. Along with the surrounding shores, it belongs to the Natura network because of its unique natural beauty.

Frangokastello

Frangokastello is one of the most famous beaches of Crete, famous for the local Venetian castle on the beautiful beach and the legendary ghosts of Drosoulites. The extensive, sheltered, and gently shelving sandy beach of Frangokastelo is truly magnificent, with sand and shallow turquoise waters.

Samaria's gorge

Along with the surrounding slopes and a number of smaller gorges branching off from it, Samaria Gorge forms the whole of the area designated as a national park, a status that offers protection to over 450 species of plant and animal, 70 of which are endemic to Crete. Samaria gorge is the longest in Europe (total length 16km), and one of the most impressive gorges.

How to get there

By plane

There are direct flights from the Athens International Airport Eleftherios Venizelos (ATH) to the airport in Chania. A number of locations in Europe and the UK also offer direct flights to Chania. London, Helsinki and Brussels are a few of the cities that fly directly to Chania. Chania's Old Town is a 20-minute drive from the airport and can be reached by taxi, private transfer, rental car or bus.

By Ferryboat

For a more leisurely way to get to Crete, you can take a fast or slow ferry from Piraeus, the main port of Athens. Ferry schedules can be more limited in the low season (mid-June through mid-September). Economy and business class seats, as well as cabins, are available for these journeys and the ferry can be a nice way to take in the wonderful sights of the Greek islands.
